What We Compete In

Two competitions.
One engineering culture.

SAE Aero Design

Design, Build, Fly for Maximum Payload

SAE Aero Design challenges teams to engineer radio-controlled aircraft that carry the greatest possible payload within a defined set of mission rules. The competition evaluates design reports, system integration, and live flight performance.

View our SAE work →
AIAA Design/Build/Fly

Mission-Driven Aircraft Design

AIAA DBF presents a new, multi-mission scoring scenario each year, requiring teams to optimise their aircraft geometry, payload, and flight profile against a unique set of technical objectives. A written report accounts for a significant portion of the final score.

View our DBF work →
The Engineering Process

From concept to flight line.

Every aircraft Arcis produces passes through a disciplined engineering cycle — simulation, prototyping, fabrication, testing, and iteration — before competition day.

01
Analyse
Aerodynamic simulation, structural analysis, and systems planning before any material is cut.
02
Design
Full CAD modelling of airframe, avionics layout, and payload mechanisms. Iterated against competition scoring.
03
Build
In-house fabrication using composites, laser cutting, 3D printing, and hand layup in the team workshop.
04
Fly
Ground testing, taxi runs, first flights, and competition day. Data collected for the next season's iteration.
